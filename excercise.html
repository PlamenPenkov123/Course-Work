Here are some basic excercises you can start of with.

Bench Press

  • Lie on a flat bench with a barbell above your chest.
  • Grip the bar slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  • Lower the bar to your chest.
  • Push the bar back up to the starting position.

Push Up

  • Start in a plank position with hands slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  • Lower your body by bending your elbows
  • Push back up to the starting position.
  • Keep your body in a straight line throughout.

Dumbbell Flyes

  • Lie on a flat bench with a dumbbell in each hand.
  • Start with arms extended above your chest.
  • Lower the dumbbells out to the sides in a wide arc..
  • Bring them back up, squeezing your chest.

Barbell Rows

  • Set up with a barbell on the floor in front of you.
  • Bend at the hips and knees to pick up the bar with an overhand grip.
  • Keep your back straight, chest up, and pull the barbell to your upper abdomen.
  • Lower the barbell back to the floor with control.

Pull Up

  • Hang from a pull-up bar with palms facing away.
  • Pull your body up until your chin clears the bar.
  • Lower your body back down with control.
  • Engage your lats and keep your core tight.

Lat Pulldowns

  • Grab the bar with your hands slightly wider than shoulder-width apart.
  • Keep your chest up and pull the bar down to your chest.
  • Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the bottom.
  • Slowly return the bar to the starting position.

Squat

  • Stand with feet shoulder-width apart.
  • Lower your body by bending your knees and hips.
  • Keep your back straight and chest up.
  • Descend until your thighs are parallel to the ground.
  • Push through your heels to return to the starting position.

Lunge

  • Take a step forward with one leg and lower your body until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle.
  • The back knee should hover just above the ground.
  • Push off the front foot to return to the starting position.
  • Repeat on the other leg.

Leg Press

  • Sit on the leg press machine with your back flat against the pad.
  • Place your feet shoulder-width apart on the platform.
  • Unrack the weight and lower the platform until your knees are bent at a 90-degree angle.
  • Push the platform back up to the starting position using the strength of your legs.
